The Eye of the Hurricane

  • by mádar hálás

    • "Do you know where those goddamn voting machines came from?"
    "That sonofabitch is going to tax you like you would not believe, and then they can pay for all the illegals' healthcare while the rest of us go bankrupt."
    "They're going door-to-door making sure people aren't having parties and taking them to jail; they're talking about enacting martial law."
    "They are going to start taxing guns that you already own, and if you don't pay, they'll come take your guns away!"
    "They want to start taking your land for redistribution!"
    "They."

    "They."

    "They!"

    The magical "They". The "They" that has been at the center of conspiracy circles for decades until recently, and has now infiltrated "their" way into the forefront of modern politics. This is not a discussion of what is conspiracy and what is not. This is a discussion of how They are being allowed to destroy the collective sanity of our population. Before we get to the crux of things, just an aside- I've been studying conspiracies from the periphery for well over a decade. I say that because there are a lot of theories to which I don't subscribe, and some that I think are only scratching the surface of the truth. I don't consider myself a conspiracy theorist, I consider myself someone who absolutely refuses to accept anything but the truth, whether it be in domestic and foreign affairs and politics, spirituality, or physics and nature. And that means a lifelong quest, and learning to simply drown out the noise of things I believe to be just that- noise.

    I feel that a vast majority of the sensory input we receive (especially now, and especially as Americans) on a daily basis is an ever-present hum of zealous faces and their shouting, exponentially-reproducing podcasts, assembly-line Youtube videos, enraged Twitter posts, divergent news channels broadcasting 24 hours a day, because how dare you miss anything at all- you just have to stay informed as to what's going on in the world. It's simply a matter of psychology; the majority of a population can easily be controlled by fear, and in this case, the Fear of Not Knowing. It's almost a reverse psychology, and a brilliant execution at that. Fear results from that which is unknown, and in this case, people are afraid of missing the latest headline or piece of "Opinion Porn" (thanks to Mitch Horowitz for that term). But the more the people ingest of these ever-consuming distractions, the more fear is being generated, and on a truly behemoth scale. If the goal is for the country to be completely de-stabilized, we are well on our way.

    In the meantime, there a tiny handful of us who like to stay informed just enough to not be ostriches and maintain an awareness, but choose not to dwell on the blistering negativity and hatred- the doom and gloom of the current state of affairs, if you will. Fixating on the viscous, apocalyptic mire that is being shoved at the people does absolutely nothing but promote de-generation. I won't get into the philosophy of New Thought, Hermetics, and Theosophy, but they are the primary wheels that drive my understanding of life and why we are all here. Some of the underlying principles are thus:
    -Your mind (mostly the subconscious) and more importantly, your imagination, are part of what is commonly known as "God". We are all connected to a cosmic consciousness that underlies all things, people, and all of creation.
    -The purpose of all life and the Universe is advancement.
    -What you dwell upon becomes your reality. It is therefore a destructive practice to maintain negative thoughts and also to immerse one's self in negative or diminishing stimuli (news, radio, social media, being around negative people, etc).

    With that, I choose not to contribute to the collective quagmire that is currently being nourished by millions upon millions giving their utmost attention to the world's problems. Many of the ancients have established cycles that spin through time (not getting into a discussion of linear vs. non-linear time here) and for the most part, all of them are in agreement that at this point in our "development", we are diving head-first into the transition period between major cycles. It is to be expected that this will be a period of great upheaval, things will change (people inherently do not like change), and I feel that the powers that be are doing everything to distract the majority from the inevitable. Our government and economic systems are completely unsustainable at this point, and as much as I don't want to reference Orwell due to the popular Orwellian over-saturation (because it's too easy of a comparison), I feel like it's necessary to mention the fictional government always creates the diversion of "Other guy bad, other situation bad, it's not us, it's them!" to keep the people from banding against them. Ultimately, the fixation on the distractions does absolutely nothing other than add to the exponentially-growing whirlpool of "bad juju" and prevents one from using their energies for advancement and good in their own life (which then contributes to the advancement of All).

    To those of us who see what is happening from a fly-on-the-wall perspective and have chosen not to be included in the 24/7 proliferation of chaos, unfortunately, a melancholy sense of ostracization and even stigmatization has been attached. Personally, in the midst of a growing sea of people who are outwardly exhibiting such despair, grief, and outrage (and it sure does seem like everybody), I feel like a complete crazy person. Maintaining my chosen levels of positivity and advancing thoughts now comes with almost a sense of guilt; "everybody else is swimming in misery, and I'm not". I have no common ground for conversation anymore, when all people want to talk about is politics, a pandemic, and how awful everything is. I can not, and will not, allow myself to join the destruction-party, because it won't help anyone, especially myself. I have spent so much effort in studying the mind, philosophy, religions, and the esoteric, and dragging myself out of the worst existential depression I've ever faced to now consider for the sake of others' acceptance that everything I believe is somehow secondary to a manufactured, shit-show carnival of the highest order.

    Here is my plea to you, dear reader:
    It is wise to be aware of the madness going on in our world. I am not detracting from that. However, it takes- at best- 5 to 10 minutes of your day to obtain the news for that day. You do not need the opinions of professional opinion-givers and pundits. You do not need the opinions of those on social media. You do not need the opinions of random faces on screens. Consider the amount of time you spend in any given day chatting with co-workers, your hair stylist, family members, etc. about all the noise, on top of all the time you spent throughout the day directly consuming it. Just give a few minutes to think about all the other things you could do with all that time that would contribute, in a creative way, to your own life, or maybe even to the greater good. Consider pro-activity instead of reactivity.

    To those few "lunatics" out there on the fringe:
    The reason I wanted to write this post was that hopefully someone else would eventually read it, and know that there are a few of us out here. We're living on our own little islands and just watching the wind, rain, and lightning swirling around the ocean but not touching our sacred little bit of sand.
